1998

Before streaming. Before playlists.
Before the algorithm. There was CL.

In 1998, while the music industry was still mailing CDs to radio stations and hoping for the best, Corey "CL" Llewellyn saw something nobody else did: DJs were the real gatekeepers. They decided what got played in the clubs, on the radio, at the parties that made records blow up. So he built a platform to get unreleased music directly into their hands — digitally, instantly, globally.

He didn't just start a company. He invented an entirely new category: digital DJ music distribution. And it changed everything.

What Happened Next

That platform took Mims to #1 on Billboard. And that was just the beginning.

DIGIWAXX became the engine behind some of the biggest breakout stories in modern music. When Columbia Records needed to launch Polo G, Yung Bleu, Fivio Foreign, Lil Tjay, and Arizona Zervas into the mainstream, CL's platform and relationships were part of the push that made it happen.

He went on to negotiate multimillion-dollar partnerships with Microsoft, Sony, Atlantic Records, and Universal-affiliated labels. He built and led a 30+ person team. He scaled to over 300 active artist and label accounts. And he did it all as an independent Black entrepreneur — no VC, no corporate parent, no safety net.

The Platform

New Music Wednesday

CL didn't stop at distribution. He wanted to create the room where it happens — a place where artists, DJs, media, labels, and culture all collide in real time. So he built New Music Wednesday: a weekly live event at The Penthouse NYC that's become one of the most dynamic independent music platforms in the game. Every week, legends and rising stars sit down with CL, perform, and get exposure to a network of 50+ DJs and 50,000+ industry contacts.
